Good morning, class. Welcome to Microwaving 101: Introduction to Microwaves. I am Professor Schwamm, and I'm Professor Carrasco.
If you have questions concerning the do's and don'ts of the microwave culinary arts, this is the course for you.
Some foods are not meant to be cooked in a microwave. Like raisins.
Marshmallows are meant for the campfire, not the microwave.
Generally, no fruits in the microwave. They can ignite and possibly implode.
Again with the fruit.
Hot peppers may catch on fire and will release fumes that burn.
Ideally, you have the common sense to not put things other than food in the microwave. However, if you feel the need, be sure to avoid heating up things like yogurt containers, which can melt because they are not designed to survive intense heat.
Let's jump right in. It is important to always cover your food before microwaving.

Now that you have covered up your food, make sure the container you are using is microwave-safe.
Unless you want to set off the fire alarm, remember: no metal in the microwave, whether it's aluminum foil...
...Or a spoon...
...Or the metal handle on your Chinese takeout box...
...Or a juice pouch. Who doesn't love warm juice?
Let's take a detour. There are good and bad ways to cook a quick breakfast. You can put an egg in the micrwave. How delicious.
